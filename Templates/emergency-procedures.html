EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

What to do before, during, and after different types of emergencies

Before Storm Surge

  • Monitor weather forecasts and storm surge warnings
  • Know your evacuation zone and routes
  • Prepare emergency kit with supplies for 72 hours
  • Secure outdoor furniture and objects
  • Review insurance policies and document belongings
  • Identify higher ground locations

During Storm Surge

  • Evacuate immediately if ordered by authorities
  • Move to higher ground or upper floors
  • Stay away from floodwater
  • Avoid walking or driving through moving water
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts
  • Stay indoors until authorities say it's safe

After Storm Surge

  • Wait for official all-clear before returning
  • Avoid floodwater - may contain debris or contaminants
  • Check for structural damage before entering buildings
  • Document damage with photos for insurance
  • Clean and disinfect everything touched by floodwater
  • Be aware of displaced wildlife and hazards

Before Landslide

  • Learn about landslide risk in your area
  • Watch for warning signs like tilting trees or poles
  • Develop evacuation plan with multiple routes
  • Keep emergency supplies ready
  • Install flexible pipe fittings for gas and water
  • Consider professional slope stability assessment

During Landslide

  • Move away from the path of landslide quickly
  • Run to nearest high ground perpendicular to flow
  • Protect your head from falling debris
  • Stay alert for flooding after landslide
  • Listen for unusual sounds indicating movement
  • Evacuate if you suspect imminent landslide

After Landslide

  • Stay away from slide area - more slides may occur
  • Check for injured or trapped persons
  • Watch for flooding or broken utility lines
  • Report broken utility lines to authorities
  • Replant damaged ground as soon as possible
  • Seek professional evaluation of land stability

Before Thunderstorm

  • Monitor weather forecasts and warnings
  • Secure outdoor objects that could blow away
  • Trim tree branches near your home
  • Install surge protectors for electronics
  • Review family emergency plan
  • Charge electronic devices and flashlights

During Thunderstorm

  • Go indoors immediately when thunder is heard
  • Stay away from windows, doors, and porches
  • Avoid electrical equipment and plumbing
  • Don't use corded phones except for emergencies
  • If outdoors, seek shelter in hard-topped vehicle
  • Avoid tall objects like trees if caught outside

After Thunderstorm

  • Wait 30 minutes after last thunder before going out
  • Check for damage to home and property
  • Avoid downed power lines and report them
  • Be cautious of flooding in low-lying areas
  • Check on neighbors, especially elderly
  • Document any damage for insurance claims

Before Typhoon

  • Monitor typhoon tracking and evacuation orders
  • Stock up on food, water, and medical supplies
  • Secure or bring in outdoor furniture
  • Board up windows with plywood
  • Fill bathtubs and containers with water
  • Fuel vehicles and check emergency equipment

During Typhoon

  • Stay indoors and away from windows
  • Go to interior room on lowest floor
  • Listen to battery-powered radio for updates
  • Avoid using candles - use flashlights instead
  • Don't go outside during eye of storm
  • Be prepared for power outages

After Typhoon

  • Wait for official all-clear before venturing out
  • Watch for flooding and storm surge
  • Avoid downed power lines and damaged buildings
  • Use generators outdoors only to prevent CO poisoning
  • Boil water if advised by authorities
  • Take photos of damage for insurance

Before Flood

  • Know your area's flood risk and evacuation routes
  • Sign up for community alert systems
  • Make flood emergency plan with family
  • Keep emergency supplies in waterproof container
  • Consider flood insurance (requires 30-day waiting period)
  • Identify higher ground locations nearby

During Flood

  • Move to higher ground immediately
  • Avoid walking in moving water
  • Don't drive through flooded roads
  • Stay away from downed power lines
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts
  • Evacuate if told to do so by authorities

After Flood

  • Return home only when authorities say it's safe
  • Avoid floodwater - may be contaminated
  • Check for structural damage before entering
  • Clean and disinfect everything touched by floodwater
  • Throw away food that came in contact with floodwater
  • Document damage with photos

Before Earthquake

  • Secure heavy furniture and appliances to walls
  • Practice "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" drills
  • Identify safe spots in each room
  • Keep emergency supplies accessible
  • Learn how to turn off gas, water, and electricity
  • Make family communication plan

During Earthquake

  • Drop to hands and knees immediately
  • Take cover under sturdy desk or table
  • Hold on to shelter and protect head/neck
  • Stay where you are until shaking stops
  • If outdoors, move away from buildings and trees
  • If driving, pull over and stop safely

After Earthquake

  • Check for injuries and provide first aid
  • Inspect home for damage and hazards
  • Turn off utilities if damaged
  • Clean up spilled hazardous materials
  • Be prepared for aftershocks
  • Stay out of damaged buildings

Before Fire

  • Install smoke alarms and check batteries monthly
  • Create and practice fire escape plan
  • Keep fire extinguishers in key locations
  • Clear vegetation around home (defensible space)
  • Use fire-resistant materials for landscaping
  • Know two ways out of every room

During Fire

  • Get out fast and call 911
  • Crawl low under smoke to avoid inhaling it
  • Feel doors before opening - don't open if hot
  • Close doors behind you as you escape
  • Meet at designated meeting place
  • Never go back inside for belongings

After Fire

  • Don't enter damaged building until cleared
  • Watch for hot spots that may re-ignite
  • Check with fire department before re-entry
  • Beware of structural damage
  • Document damage with photos
  • Contact insurance company immediately

Before Tsunami

  • Learn if you live in tsunami hazard zone
  • Know evacuation routes to higher ground
  • Practice evacuation drills with family
  • Prepare emergency kit for quick grab
  • Sign up for tsunami warning alerts
  • Identify vertical evacuation structures

During Tsunami

  • Move to higher ground immediately
  • Don't wait for official warning
  • Go as high and as far inland as possible
  • If trapped, go to upper floor of sturdy building
  • Stay away from beach and waterfront
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts

After Tsunami

  • Stay away from flooded and damaged areas
  • Wait for official all-clear before returning
  • Be aware that tsunamis come in series
  • Avoid disaster areas to allow rescue operations
  • Help injured or trapped persons if safe to do so
  • Stay out of buildings with water around them

Before Heat Emergency

  • Install air conditioning or cooling systems
  • Identify air-conditioned public places nearby
  • Check on elderly neighbors and relatives
  • Never leave people or pets in parked cars
  • Learn signs of heat-related illness
  • Prepare cooling supplies and extra water

During Heat Emergency

  • Stay indoors in air conditioning when possible
  • Drink plenty of water even if not thirsty
  • Avoid alcohol and caffeine
  • Wear lightweight, light-colored clothing
  • Take cool showers or baths
  • Limit outdoor activities to early morning/evening

After Heat Emergency

  • Continue monitoring weather forecasts
  • Check on family, friends, and neighbors
  • Watch for signs of heat exhaustion or heat stroke
  • Seek medical attention if feeling unwell
  • Gradually return to normal activities
  • Evaluate cooling strategies for future events
